How do we model intermediate materials (gray areas)?

The SIMP method

  • The consitutive tensor

    $A_{\Omega,\varepsilon} = 2\color{blue}\mu(\rho) \mathbb{I} + (\color{blue}\kappa(\rho)) - \color{blue}{\mu}(\rho)) \mathbf {I} \otimes \mathbf {I}$

    uses the following polynomial interpolation law

    $\color{blue}{\mu}(\rho) = (1-\rho^3)\mu_0 + \rho^3\mu_1 \quad \text{with} \quad \color{blue}\kappa(\rho) = (1- \rho^3)\kappa_0 + \rho^3\kappa_1 $

  • Broadly used in the community of Topology optimization
  • Incorporated in many commercial softwares
  • Inspired by a microstructure study but heuristic
  • Not always possible to understand in physical terms

Hashin-Shtrikman bounds

Rational functions

  • Definition: The set of rationals functions is defined by

    $\mathcal{R}:= \left \{t: [0,1] \rightarrow [t_0,t_1] \quad | \quad t(\rho) = \frac{A\rho^2 + B\rho + C}{D \rho + 1} \right \} $

  • Proposition: Hashin-Shtrikman Bounds are rational function with A = 0 , i.e.,

    $\mu_{LB}^{HS}, \mu_{UB}^{HS}, \kappa_{LB}^{HS}, \kappa_{UB}^{HS} \in \mathcal{R}$

H-S bounds - Topological derivative

  • Proposition: The isotropic topological derivative for the compliance can be written as
    $g_\Omega^T = \left \{ \begin{split} - e(u_\Omega):d\mathbb{C}_0:e(u_\Omega) & \quad \text{if} \in \Omega^0 \\ - e(u_\Omega):d\mathbb{C}_1:e(u_\Omega) & \quad \text{if} \in \Omega^1 \end{split} \right. $
    with
    $ \begin{split} d\mathbb{C}_0 = 2 d\mu_0 \mathbb{I} + (d\kappa_0 - d\mu_0 ) I\otimes I \\ d\mathbb{C}_1 = 2 d\mu_1 \mathbb{I} + (d\kappa_1 - d\mu_1 ) I\otimes I \end{split} $
  • Theorem: The derivative of the H-S bounds in the extreme values coincides with the topological derivative in the following sense

    $ \begin{split} \mu_{LB}^{HS}{'}(0) = d\mu_0 \\ \mu_{UB}^{HS}{'}(1) = d\mu_1 \end{split} \quad \begin{split} \kappa_{LB}^{HS} {'}(0) = d\kappa_0 \\ \kappa_{UB}^{HS} {'}(1) = d\kappa_1 \end{split} $

SIMP-ALL interpolation

  • Definition: SIMP-ALL interpolations $\mu_{SA}, \kappa_{SA} \in \mathcal{R}$ are defined as rational functions with the coeficients $A,B,C,D$ such that

    $ \left \{ \begin{split} \mu_{SA}(0) &= \mu_0 \\ \mu_{SA}(1) &= \mu_1 \\ \mu_{SA}{'}(0) &= \mu_{LB}^{HS}{'}(0) = d\mu_0 \\ \mu_{SA}{'}(1) &= \mu_{UB}^{HS}{'}(1) = d\mu_1 \end{split} \right. \quad \left \{ \begin{split} \kappa_{SA}(0) &= \kappa_0 \\ \kappa_{SA}(1) &= \kappa_1 \\ \kappa_{SA}{'}(0) &= \kappa_{LB}^{HS}{'}(0) = d\kappa_0 \\ \kappa_{SA}{'}(1) &= \kappa_{UB}^{HS}{'}(1) = d\kappa_1 \end{split} \right. $

SIMP-ALL interpolation

  • Theorem: Given two functions rational functions $ s,t \in \mathcal{R}$ such that $ s'(0) \leq t'(0)$ and $ s'(1) \geq t'(1)$ then

    $ s(\rho) \leq t(\rho) \quad \forall \rho\in [0,1] $

  • Propostion: H-S bounds satisfies the conditions of theorem before. In other words:

    $ \left \{ \begin{split} \mu_{LB}^{HS}{'}(0) \leq \mu_{UB}^{HS}{'}(0) \\ \mu_{UB}^{HS}{'}(1) \leq \mu_{LB}^{HS}{'}(1) \end{split} \right. \quad \left \{ \begin{split} \kappa_{LB}^{HS}{'}(0) &= \kappa_{UB}^{HS}{'}(0) \\ \kappa_{UB}^{HS}{'}(1) &= \kappa_{LB}^{HS}{'}(1) \end{split} \right. $

  • Corollary: The SIMP-ALL interpolation is in between H-S bounds. This is

    $ \left \{ \begin{split} \mu_{LB}^{HS}(\rho) \leq \color{blue}{\mu_{SA}^{}(\rho)} \leq \mu_{UB}^{HS}(\rho) \\ \kappa_{LB}^{HS}(\rho) \leq \color{blue}{\kappa_{SA}^{}(\rho)} \leq \kappa_{UB}^{HS}(\rho) \end{split} \right.$
