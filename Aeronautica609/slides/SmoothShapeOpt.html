Smooth shape optimization problem

Smooth shape optimization problem

  • The smooth shape optimization problem is the restriction of the density to filtered characteristic functions

    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} & \underset{\Omega \in \mathcal{U}_{ad}}{\text{min}} & J_{\varepsilon}(\Omega) = J_{\varepsilon}(\chi_{\Omega}) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
  • This problem features:
    • an approximate characteristic function: $\chi_{\Omega,\varepsilon} = \color{blue}{L_{\varepsilon}}(\chi)$
    • an approximate elasticity tensor: $A_{\Omega,\varepsilon} = 2\color{blue}\mu(\chi_{\Omega,\varepsilon}) \mathbb{I} + (\color{blue}{\kappa}(\chi_{\Omega,\varepsilon}) - \color{blue}{\mu}(\chi_{\Omega,\varepsilon})) \mathbf {I} \otimes \mathbf {I}$
    • an approximate displacement field: $u_{\Omega,\varepsilon} = u_{\chi_{\Omega,\varepsilon}}$

Smooth shape optimization problem

  • The approximate gradient $g_{\Omega,\varepsilon} = g_{\chi_{\Omega,\varepsilon}}$ is the density-based gradient $g_{\rho,\varepsilon}$ taken at $\rho=\chi_{\Omega}$ (not the shape or topological derivative). It accounts for variations of $\Omega$ by perturbations of its characteristic function:

    $\chi_{\Omega} \rightarrow \chi_{\Omega} + \rho $
  • Proposition: Let $\Omega \in \mathcal{U}_{ad}$ be a local minimizer of the cost $ J(\Omega)$ then
    1. $\forall x \in \Omega^1, g_{\Omega,\varepsilon}(x)\leq0$
    2. $\forall x \in \Gamma, g_{\Omega,\varepsilon}(x)=0$
    3. $\forall x \in \Omega^0, g_{\Omega,\varepsilon}(x)\geq0$

Filters

  • Elliptic PDE: $L_{\varepsilon}^{ell}: L^2(D) \rightarrow L^{\infty}(D)$ relies on solving

    \begin{equation*} \left \{ \begin{split}- \varepsilon^2 \Delta \rho_{\varepsilon} + \rho_{\varepsilon} &= \rho \quad {\rm in} \ D, \\ \frac{\partial \rho_{\varepsilon}}{\partial n} &= 0 \quad {\rm on} \ \partial{D}. \\\end{split} \right. \end{equation*}
  • Kernel convolution: $L_{\varepsilon}^{conv}: L^2(D) \rightarrow L^{\infty}(D)$ is defined as

    $L_{\varepsilon}^{conv}\chi_{\Omega}:= \eta_{\varepsilon} \circledast \chi_{\Omega} \quad \text{with} \quad \eta_{\varepsilon}(x) = \frac{1}{\varepsilon^d}\eta(\frac{x}{\varepsilon}) $

    where $\eta_{\varepsilon}$ is a smooth kernel with compact support and certain properties.

Convergence

  • Proposition: Regardless $L_\varepsilon $
    • $A_{\Omega,\varepsilon} \rightarrow A_{\Omega} \quad \text{in} \quad L^p(D), 1\leq p \leq \infty$
    • $u_{\Omega,\varepsilon} \rightarrow u_{\Omega} \quad \text{in} \quad W^{1,p}(D), p \geq 2$
  • Theorem: Let $\Omega \in \mathcal{U}_{ad}$ , we see that when $\quad \varepsilon \rightarrow 0$
    1. $J_{\varepsilon}(\Omega) \rightarrow J(\Omega)$
    2. $g_{\Omega,\varepsilon} \rightarrow \left \{ \begin{split} - e(u_\Omega):\mathbb{C}'(0):e(u_\Omega) & \quad \text{if} \in \Omega^0 \\ - e(u_\Omega):\mathbb{C}'(1):e(u_\Omega) & \quad \text{if} \in \Omega^1 \end{split} \right. \quad \text{in} \quad L^p(\Omega^0 \cup \Omega^1) $

      3. $ \text{with} \quad \left \{ \begin{split} \mathbb{C}'(0) = 2 \mu'(0) \mathbb{I} + (\kappa'(0) - \mu'(0) ) I\otimes I \\ \mathbb{C}'(1) = 2 \mu'(1) \mathbb{I} + (\kappa'(1)- \mu'(1) ) I\otimes I \end{split} \right.$

    3. $g_{\Omega,\varepsilon}|_{\Gamma} \rightarrow g_{\Omega}^s \quad \text{in} \quad L^1(\Gamma)$

Convergence of the cost

$C_{\varepsilon}(\Omega)= {\int_{D}{A(L_\varepsilon{\chi_{\Omega}})e(u_{\Omega,\varepsilon}):e(u_{\Omega,\varepsilon}) })}$

Convergence of the gradient in $\Omega$

(Topological derivative)
$e_{\Omega,\varepsilon}^T= \frac{\int_{\Omega}{|g_{\Omega}^{T} -g_{\Omega,\varepsilon}^{T}|^2}} {\int_{\Omega}{|g_{\Omega}^{T}|^2}}$

Convergence of the gradient in $\Gamma$

(Shape derivative)
$e_{\Omega,\varepsilon}^s= \frac{\int_{\Gamma}{|g_{\Omega}^{s} -g_{\Omega,\varepsilon}^{s}|^2}} {\int_{\Gamma}{|g_{\Omega}^{s}|^2}}$
