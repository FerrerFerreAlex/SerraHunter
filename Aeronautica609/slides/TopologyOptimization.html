Topology Optimization

Optimal Structural Design
  • Given certain structural problem (domain + boundary conditions)
  • and losing the minimum amount of stiffness, where should we remove material from?

Exact shape optimization problem

  • Formulation: find an optimal shape $\Omega \Subset D$ such that
    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} & \underset{\Omega \in \mathcal{U_{ad}}}{\text{min}} & J(\Omega):= C(\Omega) + l \ \text{Vol}(\Omega) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
  • $\mathcal{U_{ad}}$ is the set of admissible shapes (smooth).
  • $C(\Omega)$ is the compliance of the structure D, i.e.:
    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} & C(\Omega) = \int_{\Omega} A_{\Omega} e(u_{\Omega}):e(u_{\Omega}) d{\Omega} = \int_{\Gamma} g u_\Omega \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
    where $u_{\Omega}$ is solution in $H_0^1$ of (elasticity):
    $ \left \{ \begin{split}- \nabla \cdot (A_{\Omega} e(u_{\Omega})) &= f \quad {\rm in} \ \Omega, \\ u &= 0 \quad {\rm on} \ \Gamma_{D}, \\ A_{\Omega} e(u_{\Omega}) \cdot n &= g \quad {\rm on} \ \Gamma_{N}. \\\end{split} \right. \quad \text{with} \quad \color{blue}{A_{\Omega} = (1 - \chi_\Omega)A_0 + \chi_\Omega A_1} \\ $
  • $\text{Vol}(\Omega)$ is the volume of the structure: $\text{Vol}(\Omega) = \int_{\Omega} \chi_\Omega d{\Omega}$

Shape derivative

  • Hadamard's boundary variation method: $ \Omega \mapsto \Omega_{\theta} := (Id + \theta)(\Omega) $ for small $\theta \in W^{1,\infty}(\mathbb{R}^d,\mathbb{R}^d)$.
  • Definition: A functional $F(\Omega)$ is shape differentiable at $\Omega$ if the mapping $\theta \mapsto F(\theta)$ is Fr√©chet-differentiable at 0, i.e.,
    $$F(\Omega_{\theta}) = F(\Omega) + F'(\Omega)(\theta) + o(||\theta||).$$
    where in practice $\theta \in W^{1,\infty}(\mathbb{R}^d,\mathbb{R}^d)$ is restricted to the set of admissible perturbations.
  • Theorem: $C(\Omega)$ is shape differentiable at any $\Omega \in \mathcal{U}_{ad}$ with shape derivative
    $$C'(\Omega)(\theta) = \int_{\Gamma}{\color{blue}{g_{\Omega}^s} \theta \cdot n \ d\Gamma}$$

Topological derivative

  • Sensitivity of a functional when:
    • adding a small bubble: $\Omega \mapsto \Omega_{x,r} := \Omega \cup B(x,r)$ for $x \notin \overline{\Omega}$
    • nucleating a small hole: $\Omega \mapsto \Omega_{x,r} := \Omega \setminus\overline{B(x,r)}$ for $x \in \Omega$


  • Definition: A functional $F(\Omega)$ has a topological derivative at $x \in \Omega^0 \cup \Omega^1$ if there exist the following asymptotic expansion
    $$F(\Omega_{r,x}) = F(\Omega) + s_{\Omega}(x) \color{blue}{g_{\Omega}^T(x)} |B(x,r)| + o(|B(x,r)|).$$
    where $s_\Omega(x)=1$ when $x\in \Omega^0$ and $s_\Omega(x)=-1$ when $x\in \Omega^1$.

Optimality conditions

  • Definition: A shape $\Omega \in \mathcal{U}_{ad}$ is a local minimum of a function $F(\Omega)$ if
    • For any point $x \in \Omega^0 \cup \Omega^1$ for $r>0$ small enough, $F(\Omega_{x,r}) \geq F(\Omega)$
    • For any small vector field $\theta, F(\Omega_{\theta}) \geq F(\Omega) $

  • Proposition: Let $\Omega \in \mathcal{U}_{ad}$ be a local minimizer of the cost $ J(\Omega)$ then

    1. $\forall x \in \Omega^1, g_{\Omega}^T(x)\leq0$
    2. $\forall x \in \Gamma, g_{\Omega}^s(x)=0$
    3. $\forall x \in \Omega^0, g_{\Omega}^T(x)\geq0$

Smooth density optimization problem

  • We replace $\chi_{\Omega}$ by the density function $\rho \in L^{\infty}(D,[0,1])$
  • The smoothed elastic parameters are considered as:
    $\color{blue}\mu(\rho) = [1-\zeta(L_{\varepsilon}(\rho))]\mu_0 + \zeta(L_{\varepsilon}(\rho)) \mu_1 $
    $\color{blue}\kappa(\rho) = [1-\zeta(L_{\varepsilon}(\rho))]\kappa_0 + \zeta(L_{\varepsilon}(\rho)) \kappa_1 $
  • $\color{blue}{L_{\varepsilon}}: L^2(D) \rightarrow L^{\infty}(D) $ is a smoothing operator.
  • $\zeta: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ is an smooth interpolation function such that
    $\zeta(0) = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad \zeta(1) = 1$

Smooth density optimization problem

  • Formulation: find a density $\rho \in \mathcal{K}$ such that
    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} & \underset{\rho \in \mathcal{K}}{\text{min}} & J_{\varepsilon}(\rho):= C_{\varepsilon}(\rho) + l \ \text{Vol}_{\varepsilon}(\rho) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
  • $\mathcal{K}:= L^\infty(D,[0,1])$ is the closed convex subset of $L^2(D)$ of density functions
  • The approximate compliance is

    $C_\varepsilon(\rho) = \int_{D} A_{\rho,\varepsilon} e(u_{\rho,\varepsilon}):e(u_{\rho,\varepsilon}) d{\Omega} = \int_{\Gamma_N} g u_{\rho,\varepsilon}$

    where $u_{\Omega}$ is solution in $H^1$ of (elasticity):
    $ \left \{ \begin{split}- \nabla \cdot (A_{\rho,\varepsilon} e(u_{\rho,\varepsilon})) &= f \quad {\rm in} \ D, \\ u_{\rho,\varepsilon} &= 0 \quad {\rm on} \ \Gamma_{D}, \\ A_{\rho,\varepsilon} e(u_{\rho,\varepsilon}) \cdot n &= g \quad {\rm on} \ \Gamma_{N}. \\\end{split} \right. \quad \text{with} \quad \color{blue}{A_{\rho,\varepsilon} = 2\mu(\rho) \mathbb{I} + (\kappa(\rho) - \mu(\rho)) \mathbf {I} \otimes \mathbf {I}} $
  • The approximate volume of the structure: $\text{Vol}(\rho) = \int_{\Omega} L_\varepsilon (\rho) d{\Omega}$

Density-like algorithms

  • Projected gradient: One of the most natural and simple approach is to update $\rho_k $ with a projected descent direction and a line-search $\alpha $ scheme (backtracking) as follows

    $\rho_{k+1} = \text{max}(0,\text{min}(1,\rho_{k} - \alpha g_k))$

  • In practice the volume is considered as a constraint and an Augmented Lagrangian scheme is used
  • Other algorithms:
    • Ipopt: Interior point method. Large gray regions appear during the optimization process
    • MMA:Methods of moving asymptotes. So much used in the literature, not always converging

Level-set type algorithm

  • SLERP: Spherical linear interpolation (Amstutz 2006).
  • The level-set $\psi $ is defined as follows: \begin{equation*} \psi(x) = \left \{ \begin{split} < 0 \quad \text{if} \quad x \in \Omega^1 \\> 0 \quad \text{if} \quad x \in \Omega^0 \\ \end{split} \right. \end{equation*}
  • We recall that in the optimality conditions for the exact shape optimization problem, we have \begin{equation*} \left \{ \begin{split} g_{\Omega}^T < 0 \quad \text{if} \quad x \in \Omega^1 \\ g_\Omega^T> 0 \quad \text{if} \quad x \in \Omega^0 \\ \end{split} \right. \end{equation*}
  • Fixed point algorithm: the idea is to impose $\forall x \in \Omega^0 \cup \Omega^1 $

    \begin{equation*} \psi_{k+1} = g_{\Omega}^T(\psi_k) \end{equation*}

  • Usually the level-set is imposed to be bounded (i.e. $||\psi_k|| = 1$)

Level-set type algorithm

  • The update scheme is as follows: $\forall x \in \Omega^0 \cup \Omega^1 $

    \begin{equation*} \psi_{k+1} = \frac{ \sin(\kappa \theta_k)}{\sin( \theta_k)}\psi_{k} + \frac{1 - \sin(\kappa \theta_k)}{\sin( \theta_k)}g_{k} \end{equation*}

    where $\theta = \frac{(\psi,g)}{||g||}$ and $\kappa$ place the role of a line search (back-tracking).
