Algorithms

Density-like algorithms

  • Projected gradient: One of the most natural and simple approach is to update $\rho_k $ with a projected descent direction and a line-search $\alpha $ scheme (backtracking) as follows

    $\rho_{k+1} = \text{max}(0,\text{min}(1,\rho_{k} - \alpha g_k))$

  • In practice the volume is considered as a constraint and an Augmented Lagrangian scheme is used
  • Other algorithms:
    • Ipopt: Interior point method. Large gray regions appear during the optimization process
    • MMA:Methods of moving asymptotes. So much used in the literature, not always converging

Level-set type algorithm

  • SLERP: Spherical linear interpolation (Amstutz 2006).
  • The level-set $\psi $ is defined as follows: \begin{equation*} \psi(x) = \left \{ \begin{split} < 0 \quad \text{if} \quad x \in \Omega^1 \\ > 0 \quad \text{if} \quad x \in \Omega^0 \\ \end{split} \right. \end{equation*}
  • We recall that in the optimality conditions for the exact shape optimization problem, we have \begin{equation*} \left \{ \begin{split} g_{\Omega}^T < 0 \quad \text{if} \quad x \in \Omega^1 \\ g_\Omega^T > 0 \quad \text{if} \quad x \in \Omega^0 \\ \end{split} \right. \end{equation*}
  • Fixed point algorithm: the idea is to impose $\forall x \in \Omega^0 \cup \Omega^1 $

    \begin{equation*} \psi_{k+1} = g_{\Omega}^T(\psi_k) \end{equation*}

  • Usually the level-set is imposed to be bounded (i.e. $||\psi_k|| = 1$)

Level-set type algorithm

  • The update scheme is as follows: $\forall x \in \Omega^0 \cup \Omega^1 $

    \begin{equation*} \psi_{k+1} = \frac{ \sin(\kappa \theta_k)}{\sin( \theta_k)}\psi_{k} + \frac{1 - \sin(\kappa \theta_k)}{\sin( \theta_k)}g_{k} \end{equation*}

    where $\theta = \frac{(\psi,g)}{||g||}$ and $\kappa$ place the role of a line search (back-tracking).
